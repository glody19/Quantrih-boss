What is backtesting?

Backtesting applies a portfolio strategy to past market data to see how it would have performed. It does not predict the future — past returns never guarantee tomorrow's. Use it to compare strategies, understand risk (drawdowns, volatility), and stress-test your assumptions.

Monte Carlo Simulation

Project the range of possible outcomes for your portfolio with cashflows, inflation, and volatility — 10,000 simulated paths.

σ

Methodology

Monte Carlo iterates capital month by month. Each month it draws a return from a Normal distribution (compound annual return + volatility × Z), applies the cashflow (contribution or withdrawal, inflation-adjusted yearly), and floors capital at zero (plan failure). Run across thousands of paths it produces a probability distribution — not a single forecast.

Why broker choice matters

Fees compound. A 1% recurring fee can cost you 25% of your returns over 30 years. Pick a regulated broker (FCA, BaFin, FSMA, AMF…) with low fees, ETF availability, and the tax wrappers your country offers (PEA, ISA, Pension III…).

What is the Efficient Frontier?

Pioneered by Harry Markowitz (Nobel Prize 1990), the Efficient Frontier is the set of portfolios that maximize expected return for a given risk level. The Tangency Portfolio is the point with the highest Sharpe ratio — the best risk-adjusted return. Think of it as the most efficient way to allocate, given the assets you choose.
